**What**
\
\
Really Simple Syndication (RSS) is a standardized format for users and programs to send and receive updates. And as we look for alternatives ways to connect online, RSS feeds are options that give us back control over what we are presented with, and claw back data that we don’t want to give away through aggressive tracking that now pervades most online platforms. 
\
\
RSS is the acronym for the cute name: [Really Simple Syndication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSS) (or Rich Site Summary?). It _is_ really simple! RSS is technically a file on your website / blog that lists and structures the content of it with a bunch of metadata, such as title, date of submission, and the URL that leads to the actual content. RSS is very particular with how this data is structured. The standardization of data is really what makes it so smart. It is guaranteed to come in a very particular format, anyone can make a program that reads any RSS and use it for any kind of project. Reading RSS will never change the original. 
\
\
Because RSS is so convenient for passing information between websites, its use has become widespread to the point that it really is _the_ vehicle for sharing content for the web. Chances are you have been consuming media via RSS feeds for a very long time already, even if you're not aware of it. RSS has been the dominant web feed that distributes podcasts [since it added the option to carry audio files in 2000](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSS#:~:text=In%20December%202000%2C%20Winer%20released%20RSS%200.92%5B18%5D%20a%20minor%20set%20of%20changes%20aside%20from%20the%20introduction%20of%20the%20enclosure%20element%2C%20which%20permitted%20audio%20files%20to%20be%20carried%20in%20RSS%20feeds%20and%20helped%20spark%20podcasting.%20He%20also%20released%20drafts%20of%20RSS%200.93%20and%20RSS%200.94%20that%20were%20subsequently%20withdrawn.%5B19%5D). When you want to listen to a show, you add the RSS feed to your podcast client, and the client can then list available episodes and download or stream them for listening or viewing. This is really the gist of it whether for podcasts, blogposts, news or youtube videos.
\
\
![Screenshot](/images/8/Screenshot%202026-04-24%20at%2013.51.02.png)
\
\
The popularity of RSS stems from the convenience of sharing information in a reliable and standardized format and how widespread it is. Regular folks may not know what it is (and [look at this graph](https://trends.google.com/explore?q=RSS&date=2005-01-01%202026-04-24) on the seach for "RSS" on Google from 2005 to today - not looking good), but its popularity is self-reinforcing. Even if RSS stops being used by consumers altogether, it will most likely be powering the back end of many websites and programs that primarily collect content from other places for a long time to come. **The humble RSS protocol is alive and well!**

**Why**
\
\
Ok cool cool, but why should *I* care about RSS and invest precious life energy into this? 
\
\
We are spending [more](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c39prelx2mxo) time online than ever before, we keep spending [even more](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c39prelx2mxo), and it is probably making us [miserable](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/screen-time-and-subjective-well-being_c840403c-en/full-report.html). Some of you might be using that time for [nice things](https://ebird.org/home) but most of us pass hours everyday anxiously stuck inside services that increasingly limit external links and really our free movement on the internet. Platforms like Facebook/Instagram and Twitter all actively keep you from wandering the web like a [digital garden](https://digitalgarden.hypha.coop/rss) and instead treat you liker a virtual prisoner to the endless doomscroll. A vanishingly small amount of content on your FYP is now from accounts you actually follow. We are foie-grass-duck-[stuffed](https://www.404media.co/ai-job-loss-research-ignores-how-ai-is-utterly-destroying-the-internet/) with aggregate snippets of information in AI 'overviews' and LLM summaries. Everything already went to hell in a [handbasket](https://www.bloodinthemachine.com/p/ais-aesthetics-of-failure). It gets harder and harder to find quality writing, creative expression and the output of people you care about and want to follow. 
\
\
Of course you can subscribe to newsletters, and thats a wonderful way to circumvent the terrible-ness of the internet and listen directly to voices that you choose yourself. But increasingly every technology is spying on you and harvesting data on your behaviour to sell to the open market of postmodern ad-personalization. I don't mean every newsletter writer is doing this, but the platforms they let themselves and their intellectual property get locked into may well be. Apps like Substack platform literal [nazis](https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2023/11/substack-extremism-nazi-white-supremacy-newsletters/676156/?gift=j9r7avb6p-KY8zdjhsiSZ1RLcOPmvywfPzJWyac8ltI) as well as collect your data to regurgitate back an algorithmic feed full of ads. Patreon is full of nonconsensual [AI porn](https://www.404media.co/how-makers-of-nonconsensual-ai-porn-make-a-living-on-patreon/) of real victims. Large commercial platforms routinely let us down and choose profit above all else (I use [Ghost](https://ghost.org/), an open source alternative).
\
\
Yady yady it all [sucks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_Internet_theory), we know! But what if there was a way to fight back against the dull violence of the algo, to decide yourself what content you want to see and which voices to listen to, without being tracked, surveilled, smothered in AI and followed by sponsored ad content weaponized against your most sensitive data that has been extracted from you without consent? Good news, RSS makes the web [bearable](https://doctorow.medium.com/https-pluralistic-net-2026-03-07-reader-mode-personal-disenshittification-bbbc2c6cf442).

**How**
\
\
Firstly you need an RSS reader: the place where you read all you news. If we think back to the podcast example, you need a place to listen to podcasts and you have a choice and can pick the one you like, can afford, or that fits you device of choice.  I pay for a subscription with [Feedbin](https://feedbin.com/), which I really like. Here's a couple of suggestions for free readers: IOS app [Feeed](https://feeeed.nateparrott.com/), [Lire](https://lireapp.com/), [Vienna](https://www.vienna-rss.com/), [Inoreader](https://www.inoreader.com/). Don't worry too much about choosing. These are all fine. As [Molly White](https://www.citationneeded.news/curate-with-rss/) put it: "RSS is a protocol, and switching feed readers later is straightforward."

Once you've picked your reader you need to add all the feeds you want to follow. Most readers will allow you to add new RSS feeds via a URL. You can simply paste the URL of your newsletter of choice (such as [this](https://www.kaerecomputer.dk/rss.xml) one!). You can also add a traditional news publication, a blog, a youtube channel, a Bluesky or Mastodon feed or even a [subreddit you enjoy](https://www.reddit.com/r/TreesSuckingOnThings/). The world is your oyster! Molly made a really nice [blogroll for inspiration](https://www.mollywhite.net/blogroll/) that is heavily into tech and neo-luddite activism. I'll have to make one for myself soon. My list is currently a mix of tech criticism, antifascist gardening and knitting and sewing blogs.

**AHHHHH!**
\
\
When I finally started using a RSS reader it felt like instant relief. "It’s almost impossible to overstate how *superior* RSS is to the median web page", as [Cory](https://doctorow.medium.com/https-pluralistic-net-2026-03-07-reader-mode-personal-disenshittification-bbbc2c6cf442) Doctorow wrote. No more distractions or disruptions, [dark design patterns](https://userinyerface.com/), no inbox full of guilt-inducing prompts that pull my focus from the newsletter or article I'm trying to enjoy. What this new quietude did to me felt *profound*. Since switching to reading newsletters and blogs in RSS format, I have been reading more than ever. I have been engaging with the material in a deeper way, and in return felt more inspired to create. RSS causes less stress, and allowed me rediscover how much I love [reading](https://gilest.org/notes/rss-feels.html).

This is a new way of engaging with the web. You're not being bombarded with information or looking for the needle (person you follow) in the haystack (algorithm). Information is a stream of media that you can enjoy at anytime you like.

**Support**
\
\
The only flipside to all of this is how much it emphasizes our responsibility to support writers and creators we care about, whenever possible. The RSS feed severs a kind of bond that used to exist between newsletter writer and reader. If you read this text for example in an RSS reader, I'll never know about it. I added the RSS stream to my blog and now anyone may 'subscribe' to it without me ever knowing about it. In our crumbling old-media-landscape authors use their social media numbers to land book deals or paid writing gigs. Its understood that even if you don't support an independent writer monetarily, you're still vaguely doing 'something' by juicing their numbers on social media with engagement. I'm keenly aware that when I unsubscribe my email in Substack and resubscribe in RSS, I no longer count as metric the writer can meaningfully leverage in any kind of way. I don't have a good answer to fix this problem other than to sign up for a paid subscription or make a one-time contribution to writers that I regularly read. I have mixed feelings about this, but I can honestly tell you, dear reader, than I'd be delighted for you to read my newsletter as an RSS feed. You mean more to me than data, sweet pea!
